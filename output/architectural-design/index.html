Architectural design

The process of defining a structured solution that meets all of the technical and operational requirements of a system.

Description

Architectural design in the context of Information Assurance Management (IAM) refers to the comprehensive framework that outlines the structural components, relationships, and guidelines necessary for the design and implementation of secure and effective information systems. This process involves analyzing the functional requirements of the system, identifying the necessary security controls, and ensuring compliance with relevant regulations and standards. Architectural design encompasses various layers, including physical, logical, and application architectures, each playing a critical role in safeguarding information assets. It also considers scalability, maintainability, and resilience against potential threats. Effective architectural design not only addresses current security needs but also anticipates future challenges, providing a roadmap for evolution in response to changing technologies and threat landscapes.

Examples

  • Designing a multi-tier web application architecture with secure data access layers.
  • Creating a cloud architecture that incorporates identity management and secure access controls.

Additional Information

  • Architectural design must align with organizational policies and compliance requirements.
  • It often involves collaboration between IT, security teams, and business units to ensure all perspectives are considered.
