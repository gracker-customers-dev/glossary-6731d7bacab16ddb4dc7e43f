Multi-Agent System

A system composed of multiple interacting intelligent agents.

Description

A Multi-Agent System (MAS) refers to a computational framework that involves multiple autonomous entities, known as agents, which interact with each other to achieve individual or collective goals. Each agent is capable of perceiving its environment, reasoning about its actions, and acting upon that environment. These agents can be homogenous or heterogeneous, meaning they can be similar in functionality or diverse in capabilities and roles. MAS is often used to solve complex problems that are difficult for a single agent or traditional computing systems to address due to their dynamic and distributed nature. Applications of Multi-Agent Systems range from robotics, where multiple robots coordinate to accomplish tasks, to complex simulations in traffic management, environmental monitoring, and distributed control systems. The agents in a MAS can communicate, negotiate, and collaborate, allowing for more robust and scalable solutions to problems that require a degree of cooperation and coordination among entities.

Examples

  • Robotic swarms working together for search and rescue operations
  • Distributed sensor networks monitoring environmental conditions

Additional Information

  • Agents can be programmed with varying degrees of intelligence and autonomy.
  • MAS can be applied in fields such as artificial intelligence, economics, and social sciences.
