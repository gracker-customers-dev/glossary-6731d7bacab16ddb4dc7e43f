Audit sampling

The process of selecting a subset of items from a population to evaluate and draw conclusions about the entire group.

Description

Audit sampling is a technique used in the field of auditing and assurance to evaluate a subset of a larger population. This method allows auditors to gather evidence and form conclusions regarding the accuracy and reliability of financial statements or compliance with regulations without having to examine every single item. The primary goal of audit sampling is to obtain a representative sample that reflects the characteristics of the entire population. This approach is crucial because it saves time and resources while still enabling auditors to identify potential issues or anomalies. There are two main types of audit sampling: statistical sampling, where the auditor uses mathematical techniques to select the sample, and non-statistical sampling, where the selection is based on the auditor's judgment. The results from the sample are then extrapolated to estimate the characteristics of the entire population, allowing for efficient audits that maintain a high level of assurance.

Examples

  • An auditor selects a random sample of 100 transactions from a total of 10,000 to test for accuracy.
  • A compliance audit uses stratified sampling to review specific segments of a company’s records to ensure adherence to regulatory standards.

Additional Information

  • Audit sampling can help identify trends or irregularities in financial data.
  • Effective sampling methods can enhance the reliability of audit findings and conclusions.
