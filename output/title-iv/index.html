Title IV

Title IV refers to a section of the Higher Education Act that governs federal student financial aid programs.

Description

Title IV of the Higher Education Act of 1965 is a crucial component of U.S. higher education policy, primarily focusing on federal student financial aid programs. This legislation provides the framework for federal funding for various types of financial assistance to students pursuing postsecondary education. Under Title IV, eligible institutions can participate in federal student aid programs, which include Pell Grants, Federal Supplemental Educational Opportunity Grants (FSEOG), Federal Work-Study, and federal student loans such as Direct Loans and PLUS Loans. The intent of Title IV is to make higher education accessible to a broader range of students, particularly those from low-income backgrounds. Institutions must meet specific eligibility requirements to participate in these programs, which are designed to ensure that students can receive the financial support they need to afford college. Title IV also establishes regulations for the administration of these financial aid programs, including student eligibility criteria, application processes, and institutional responsibilities. Overall, Title IV plays a vital role in shaping the landscape of financial aid in the United States.

Examples

  • Pell Grants for low-income undergraduate students.
  • Federal Direct Loans for student borrowing.

Additional Information

  • Institutions must be accredited to participate in Title IV programs.
  • Title IV funds must be managed according to federal regulations.
