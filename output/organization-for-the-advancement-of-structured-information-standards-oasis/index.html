Organization for the Advancement of Structured Information Standards (OASIS)

OASIS is a nonprofit consortium that drives the development, convergence, and adoption of open standards for the global information society.

Description

The Organization for the Advancement of Structured Information Standards (OASIS) is a leading international standards development organization that focuses on promoting open standards for a range of information technologies. Established in 1993, OASIS has played a pivotal role in creating standards that enable interoperability and facilitate the exchange of information across diverse systems. OASIS standards are widely used in various domains, including cloud computing, security, web services, and data formats. By fostering collaboration among industry experts, users, and stakeholders, OASIS supports the development of standards that enhance the efficiency and effectiveness of IT solutions. The organization operates through various technical committees, where members collaborate on projects to draft, refine, and publish specifications that meet the needs of the community. Notable standards developed by OASIS include the Security Assertion Markup Language (SAML) for single sign-on and the Business Process Execution Language (BPEL) for web services. OASIS's commitment to open standards helps ensure that technology remains accessible and promotes innovation across industries.

Examples

  • Security Assertion Markup Language (SAML) for federated identity management.
  • Business Process Execution Language (BPEL) for orchestrating web services.

Additional Information

  • OASIS members include a diverse range of companies, governments, and individuals.
  • The organization supports a variety of initiatives related to cybersecurity, cloud computing, and more.
