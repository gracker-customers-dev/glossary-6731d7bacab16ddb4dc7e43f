Assignable cause of process variation

A specific, identifiable source of variation in a process that can be traced back to a distinct cause.

Description

In the context of process management and quality control, an assignable cause of process variation refers to variations that can be linked to a specific factor or event. Unlike common causes of variation, which are inherent to the process and are typically random and predictable, assignable causes indicate that there is something unusual happening that has disrupted the normal operation. Identifying and addressing these causes is crucial for improving process performance and ensuring quality outcomes. Examples of assignable causes include equipment malfunction, operator error, changes in materials, or fluctuations in environmental conditions. By analyzing these causes, organizations can implement corrective actions to eliminate the sources of variation, leading to enhanced consistency and reliability in processes. This concept is fundamental in methodologies such as Six Sigma and Total Quality Management, where the goal is to minimize variation and improve overall quality.

Examples

  • A sudden spike in defects due to a malfunctioning machine.
  • Increased processing time caused by a temporary shortage of raw materials.

Additional Information

  • Assignable causes can be detected using control charts.
  • Root cause analysis is often employed to investigate and resolve assignable causes.
