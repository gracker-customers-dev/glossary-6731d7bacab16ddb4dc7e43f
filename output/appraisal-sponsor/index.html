Appraisal sponsor

An individual or organization that initiates and oversees an appraisal process.

Description

In the context of the IAM Glossary, an appraisal sponsor is a key figure who is responsible for initiating and supporting the appraisal of assets, processes, or systems within an organization. This role is crucial as the appraisal sponsor helps to define the objectives and scope of the appraisal, ensuring that it aligns with the organizationâ€™s strategic goals and compliance requirements. The sponsor may also provide necessary resources, such as funding and personnel, to facilitate the appraisal process. Furthermore, the sponsor plays an important part in communicating the value and importance of the appraisal to stakeholders, thereby fostering support and engagement throughout the organization. Their involvement can significantly influence the outcomes of the appraisal, as they help to set the tone and expectations for the assessment. By championing the appraisal process, the appraisal sponsor ensures that the findings and recommendations from the appraisal are taken seriously and acted upon, leading to improvements and enhancements in the organization's operations.

Examples

  • A senior manager who commissions a performance appraisal for a team.
  • An executive who oversees a financial audit appraisal to ensure compliance with regulations.

Additional Information

  • The appraisal sponsor may also be involved in follow-up actions based on appraisal findings.
  • Effective communication skills are essential for an appraisal sponsor to influence stakeholder buy-in.
