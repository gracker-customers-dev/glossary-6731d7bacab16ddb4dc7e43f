OpenID Foundation (OIDF)

An organization that promotes and supports the OpenID authentication standard.

Description

The OpenID Foundation (OIDF) is a nonprofit organization that facilitates the development and adoption of the OpenID authentication protocols. OpenID is an open standard that allows users to be authenticated by cooperating sites using a third-party service, eliminating the need for multiple usernames and passwords. The foundation was established to support the growth of OpenID as a secure and efficient method for identity verification on the web, fostering interoperability among different identity providers and relying parties. OIDF works on various related projects, including OpenID Connect, which is a simple identity layer on top of the OAuth 2.0 protocol. By providing a standardized approach to identity management, the OpenID Foundation enhances user experience, security, and privacy across online platforms. The foundation also engages with stakeholders from various sectors, including technology companies, standards organizations, and privacy advocates, to ensure that OpenID remains relevant and effective in addressing contemporary identity challenges.

Examples

  • OpenID Connect implementation by Google for sign-in services.
  • Use of OpenID in various web applications for single sign-on (SSO).

Additional Information

  • OIDF promotes best practices for identity and access management.
  • Membership includes major tech companies and organizations dedicated to enhancing online security.
