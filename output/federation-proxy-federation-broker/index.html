Federation Proxy / Federation Broker

A Federation Proxy or Federation Broker is a service that facilitates authentication and identity federation between different identity providers and service providers.

Description

In the context of Identity and Access Management (IAM), a Federation Proxy or Federation Broker acts as an intermediary that enables users to authenticate across different domains or organizations without needing to create multiple accounts. This is achieved by leveraging standards such as SAML (Security Assertion Markup Language) or OAuth. The Federation Broker collects authentication assertions from various identity providers to validate the user’s identity and then forwards that information to the service provider, allowing for seamless access to resources. This model enhances user experience by enabling Single Sign-On (SSO) capabilities, while also maintaining security and compliance across disparate systems. Organizations often deploy federation proxies to streamline access management, reduce administrative overhead, and enhance collaboration with partners, vendors, or other third-party services. By centralizing authentication processes, these brokers can also enforce consistent security policies and identity governance across the federation.

Examples

  • A company uses a Federation Broker to allow employees to access partner services using their corporate credentials.
  • An educational institution employs a Federation Proxy to enable students to log into various third-party educational resources with a single set of login credentials.

Additional Information

  • Federation Brokers can support multiple authentication methods, including multi-factor authentication for enhanced security.
  • They play a critical role in achieving interoperability among different identity systems and ensuring compliance with data protection regulations.
