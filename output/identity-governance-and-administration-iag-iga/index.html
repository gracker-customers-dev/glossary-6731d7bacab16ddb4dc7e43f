Identity Governance and Administration (IAG / IGA)

IAG/IGA refers to the processes and technologies that ensure the proper management of user identities and their access rights across an organization.

Description

Identity Governance and Administration (IAG / IGA) encompasses the policies, processes, and technologies that enable organizations to manage digital identities and their access to resources effectively. IGA solutions help organizations ensure compliance with regulatory requirements, improve security by enforcing least privilege access, and streamline user provisioning and de-provisioning processes. IGA includes features such as access certification, role management, policy enforcement, and workflow automation. By implementing IGA, organizations can gain visibility into user access rights, reduce the risk of unauthorized access, and ensure that users have the appropriate access for their roles. The governance aspect focuses on establishing and maintaining policies regarding identity and access management, while the administration part deals with the operational aspects of managing user identities and their access to systems and applications. Overall, IGA is vital for a robust Identity and Access Management (IAM) strategy, enabling organizations to protect sensitive data and maintain regulatory compliance.

Examples

  • Automated access reviews to ensure users have the appropriate permissions.
  • Role-based access control (RBAC) to simplify user access management.

Additional Information

  • IAG solutions often integrate with existing IAM systems for enhanced functionality.
  • Compliance with standards like GDPR and HIPAA can be facilitated through effective IGA practices.
