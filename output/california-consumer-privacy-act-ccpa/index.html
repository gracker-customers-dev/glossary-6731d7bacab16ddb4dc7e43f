California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)

A landmark privacy law enacted in California that enhances privacy rights and consumer protection for residents of California.

Description

The California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), which took effect on January 1, 2020, is a significant piece of legislation aimed at improving the privacy rights of California residents. It grants consumers the ability to know what personal information is being collected about them, the purposes for which that information is used, and the right to request deletion of their personal data. Additionally, the CCPA allows consumers to opt-out of the sale of their personal information to third parties. Businesses are required to provide clear notices regarding data collection practices and ensure that consumers can easily access their rights under the law. The CCPA applies to for-profit entities that do business in California and meet certain criteria, such as having gross revenues over a specific threshold or collecting personal data from a certain number of consumers. This law marked a significant shift towards greater accountability and transparency in data handling practices and has influenced similar privacy legislation across the United States.

Examples

  • A consumer requests a business to disclose what personal data it has collected about them.
  • A consumer opts out of having their data sold to third parties for marketing purposes.

Additional Information

  • The CCPA applies to businesses that meet certain revenue or data collection thresholds.
  • Violations of the CCPA can result in fines and legal actions against businesses.
