World Wide Web Consortium (W3C)

An international community that develops open standards to ensure the long-term growth of the Web.

Description

The World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) is an international organization founded in 1994 by Tim Berners-Lee, the inventor of the World Wide Web. Its primary mission is to lead the Web to its full potential by developing protocols and guidelines that ensure the long-term growth and interoperability of the Web. The W3C brings together various stakeholders, including businesses, developers, and researchers, to collaborate on creating standards that promote web accessibility, usability, and security. Some of the key areas of focus include HTML, CSS, XML, and various web accessibility initiatives. By establishing these standards, the W3C aims to ensure that the Web remains a universal medium for communication and collaboration, enabling all users to access and share information regardless of their devices or locations. The W3C also conducts outreach and educational activities to foster understanding and implementation of web standards across the global community.

Examples

  • HTML5 specification developed by W3C
  • Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) for making web content accessible

Additional Information

  • W3C has over 400 member organizations worldwide
  • The organization is involved in numerous initiatives addressing emerging web technologies
