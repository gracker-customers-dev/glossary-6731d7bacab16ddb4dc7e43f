Segregation of Duties (SoD)

A security principle that ensures no individual has control over all aspects of any critical transaction.

Description

Segregation of Duties (SoD) is a fundamental principle in identity and access management (IAM) aimed at reducing the risk of fraud and error by dividing responsibilities among different individuals. The core idea is that critical tasks should not be assigned to a single person, as this could lead to misuse of power or oversight. For example, in financial transactions, the person who initiates a payment should not be the same person who approves it, nor should they be the one who reconciles the account. This separation ensures that no single individual can execute a transaction without oversight, thus providing a check-and-balance system. In practice, SoD is implemented through role-based access control (RBAC) and policies that delineate which roles have access to which functions. Organizations often conduct risk assessments to identify areas where SoD is necessary and implement controls to enforce these separations. Providing training and awareness about the importance of SoD is also crucial in fostering a culture of compliance and security within the organization.

Examples

  • A finance department where one employee processes payments while another approves them.
  • In a software development environment, the developer who writes code should not be the one who deploys it to production.

Additional Information

  • SoD is essential for compliance with regulations such as Sarbanes-Oxley (SOX) and ISO 27001.
  • Automated tools can help manage and enforce SoD policies by monitoring access and alerting on potential conflicts.
