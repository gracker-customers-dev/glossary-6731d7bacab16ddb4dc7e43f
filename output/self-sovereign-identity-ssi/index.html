Self-Sovereign Identity (SSI)

A digital identity model where individuals have control over their personal data and can manage their identity without relying on a centralized authority.

Description

Self-Sovereign Identity (SSI) is a concept in identity management that allows individuals to own, control, and share their personal information without depending on third-party intermediaries. In traditional identity systems, personal data is often stored and managed by centralized authorities, such as governments or corporations, which can lead to privacy concerns and data breaches. SSI addresses these issues by enabling users to create and maintain their digital identity using decentralized technologies, such as blockchain. Users can issue, store, and present their credentials in a secure manner, maintaining full control over who has access to their information. This model not only enhances privacy and security but also empowers individuals by allowing them to selectively share their data according to their preferences. SSI can be applied in various contexts, including online authentication, access to services, and digital transactions, fostering a more user-centric approach to identity management.

Examples

  • A user creates a digital wallet that stores their verified credentials, such as education degrees and professional licenses.
  • An individual shares their identity proof with a service provider without revealing unnecessary personal information, like their address or date of birth.

Additional Information

  • SSI relies on decentralized identifiers (DIDs) and verifiable credentials to enable secure identity management.
  • The adoption of SSI can significantly reduce identity fraud and streamline user verification processes.
