Personally Identifiable Information (PII)

Information that can be used to identify an individual.

Description

Personally Identifiable Information (PII) refers to any data that could potentially be used to identify a specific individual. This can include a wide range of information, from names and addresses to more sensitive details like Social Security numbers, financial account numbers, and biometric data. In the context of Identity and Access Management (IAM), PII is critical because it is often the target of unauthorized access and data breaches. Protecting PII is essential for maintaining privacy and complying with regulations such as GDPR and HIPAA. Organizations must implement strong security measures, including data encryption and access controls, to safeguard this information. Additionally, understanding what constitutes PII helps organizations in their risk assessments and in developing policies regarding data handling and user privacy. Overall, the protection of PII is not only a legal obligation but also a crucial aspect of building trust with customers and users.

Examples

  • Full name (e.g., John Doe)
  • Email address (e.g., john.doe@example.com)
  • Social Security number (e.g., 123-45-6789)
  • Phone number (e.g., (123) 456-7890)
  • Home address (e.g., 123 Main St, Anytown, USA)

Additional Information

  • PII can be categorized into two types: sensitive PII (e.g., Social Security numbers, financial information) and non-sensitive PII (e.g., names, email addresses).
  • Organizations are required to implement data protection measures to minimize the risk of PII exposure during data collection, storage, and transmission.
