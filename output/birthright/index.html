Birthright

A set of access rights and privileges granted to users upon account creation or onboarding.

Description

In the context of Identity and Access Management (IAM), 'Birthright' refers to the predefined access rights and entitlements that are automatically assigned to a user when they are created in the system. This concept ensures that users receive the necessary permissions to perform their job functions effectively without requiring manual intervention from administrators. Birthright access is often determined by the user's role, department, or location within the organization, and is crucial for maintaining security and efficiency in managing user identities. By establishing a clear set of birthright entitlements, organizations can streamline the onboarding process, reduce errors, and ensure compliance with internal policies and external regulations. The use of birthright access can also enhance user experience, as employees can access the resources they need from day one, facilitating a smoother transition into their new roles.

Examples

  • An employee in the finance department is automatically granted access to financial systems and reporting tools upon account creation.
  • A new software developer receives access to development environments and code repositories relevant to their projects as part of their birthright access.

Additional Information

  • Birthright access can be customized based on organizational policies and needs.
  • Regular audits of birthright access can help prevent privilege creep and ensure that users only retain necessary permissions.
