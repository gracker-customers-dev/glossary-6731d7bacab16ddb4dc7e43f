Just-in-Time (JIT) Provisioning

A method of creating user accounts and granting access rights at the moment they are needed.

Description

Just-in-Time (JIT) Provisioning is an Identity and Access Management (IAM) approach that focuses on creating user accounts and assigning access rights dynamically at the time of need, rather than pre-provisioning them. This method enhances security by ensuring that users only have access to resources for the duration they require it, minimizing the risk of unauthorized access. JIT provisioning often works in conjunction with Single Sign-On (SSO) and can streamline the onboarding process by reducing administrative overhead. For instance, when a user attempts to access a system or application for the first time, JIT provisioning will automatically create their user account and assign the appropriate permissions based on their role, organizational policies, or predefined rules. This mechanism supports scalability and flexibility in managing user access, particularly in environments where users frequently change roles or where temporary access is necessary. By limiting access to only what is necessary and for the time it is needed, JIT provisioning plays a crucial role in maintaining a secure and efficient IAM strategy.

Examples

  • A new employee accesses a cloud application for the first time, and their account is created with specific permissions based on their job role.
  • A contractor needs temporary access to a project management tool, and JIT provisioning grants them access for the duration of their contract.

Additional Information

  • JIT provisioning can significantly reduce the administrative burden of managing user accounts.
  • It enhances compliance by ensuring that access rights are only granted when necessary, aligning with the principle of least privilege.
