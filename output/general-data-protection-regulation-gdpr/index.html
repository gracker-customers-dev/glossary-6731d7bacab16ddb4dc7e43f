General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

A comprehensive data protection law in the EU that governs the processing of personal data.

Description

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a regulation enacted by the European Union (EU) on May 25, 2018, aimed at enhancing individuals' control and rights over their personal data. It applies to any organization that processes personal data of EU residents, regardless of where the organization is located. The GDPR establishes strict guidelines for data collection, storage, and processing, requiring organizations to obtain explicit consent from individuals before using their data. Key principles include transparency, data minimization, accuracy, storage limitation, and accountability. Organizations must also implement appropriate security measures to protect personal data and report data breaches within 72 hours. The regulation empowers individuals with rights such as the right to access their data, the right to rectify inaccuracies, the right to erasure (the right to be forgotten), and the right to data portability. Non-compliance with GDPR can result in significant fines, making it crucial for organizations to align their data handling practices with these regulations.

Examples

  • A company must obtain explicit consent from users before sending marketing emails.
  • Individuals have the right to request deletion of their personal data stored by a service provider.

Additional Information

  • GDPR applies to both data processors and data controllers.
  • Penalties for non-compliance can reach up to €20 million or 4% of annual global turnover, whichever is higher.
