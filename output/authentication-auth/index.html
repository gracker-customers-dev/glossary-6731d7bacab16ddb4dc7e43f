Authentication (Auth)

The process of verifying the identity of a user or system.

Description

Authentication is a fundamental aspect of Identity and Access Management (IAM) that involves confirming the identity of an entity attempting to access a system, application, or resource. It typically requires users to provide credentials, such as passwords, biometric data, or security tokens, to prove their identity. The goal of authentication is to ensure that only authorized individuals or systems are allowed access to sensitive information and resources. There are several methods of authentication, including single-factor authentication (SFA), which relies on one form of verification, and multi-factor authentication (MFA), which combines multiple verification methods to enhance security. MFA can include a combination of something the user knows (like a password), something the user has (like a smartphone app or hardware token), or something the user is (such as biometric data). Effective authentication processes help to mitigate the risks of unauthorized access and data breaches, making it a critical component of a robust security framework.

Examples

  • Using a username and password to log into an online banking account.
  • Employing fingerprint recognition to unlock a smartphone.

Additional Information

  • Authentication can be classified into various types, including basic, two-factor, and biometric authentication.
  • The effectiveness of authentication measures can significantly impact overall cybersecurity posture.
