New York cybersecurity law (NY CCR)

A set of regulations aimed at enhancing the cybersecurity posture of financial services companies operating in New York.

Description

The New York Cybersecurity Regulation (NY CCR), formally known as 23 NYCRR 500, was enacted by the New York State Department of Financial Services (NYDFS) to establish a comprehensive cybersecurity framework for financial institutions. The regulation requires covered entities, including banks, insurance companies, and other financial services firms, to implement robust cybersecurity measures to protect consumer information and financial data. Key provisions of the NY CCR include the necessity for organizations to appoint a Chief Information Security Officer (CISO), conduct annual risk assessments, maintain a written cybersecurity policy, and ensure the implementation of controls to protect against data breaches and cyber threats. The regulation also mandates reporting of significant cybersecurity events within a specific timeframe. By establishing these requirements, the NY CCR aims to mitigate risks associated with cyber threats and enhance the overall security of the financial services sector in New York, thus promoting consumer confidence in the safety of their financial transactions and personal information.

Examples

  • A bank implementing a cybersecurity framework that includes regular employee training on security protocols.
  • An insurance company conducting annual risk assessments to identify vulnerabilities in its IT infrastructure.

Additional Information

  • Non-compliance can lead to significant fines and legal repercussions.
  • The regulation serves as a model for other states considering similar cybersecurity laws.
