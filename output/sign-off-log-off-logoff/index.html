Sign off/Log off (Logoff)

The process of terminating a user session on a system or application.

Description

Sign off, also known as log off or logoff, refers to the action undertaken by a user to terminate their active session on a computer, application, or online service. This process is crucial for maintaining security and privacy, as it ensures that others cannot access the userâ€™s account or sensitive information without proper authentication. When a user logs off, all active processes associated with their session are closed, and any temporary files may be deleted. Logging off can be done voluntarily by the user or automatically by the system after a period of inactivity. In the context of Identity and Access Management (IAM), logging off is an essential practice to prevent unauthorized access and is often part of compliance requirements. Users are encouraged to log off from shared or public devices to safeguard their personal information and data integrity.

Examples

  • A user logs off from their social media account on a public computer to ensure their account remains secure.
  • An employee signs off from their corporate network after completing their workday to protect sensitive company data.

Additional Information

  • Some applications offer an automatic logoff feature after a specified time of inactivity.
  • Logging off is different from simply closing a browser window as it properly ends the session.
