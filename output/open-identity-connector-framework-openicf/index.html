Open Identity Connector Framework (OpenICF)

A framework designed to facilitate the integration of identity and access management systems with various identity data sources.

Description

The Open Identity Connector Framework (OpenICF) is an open-source framework that provides a standardized approach for connecting identity management solutions to various identity data sources. It is primarily used in the context of Identity and Access Management (IAM) to streamline the process of provisioning, managing, and synchronizing identity information across different systems. The framework supports the development of connectors that can interact with diverse identity stores, such as databases, directories, and cloud services. By utilizing OpenICF, organizations can enhance their identity management capabilities, reduce the complexity of integrating disparate systems, and ensure consistent identity data across their environments. OpenICF promotes interoperability and reusability of connectors, enabling quicker deployment of identity management solutions while adhering to security and compliance requirements. Its modular architecture allows developers to create custom connectors tailored to specific requirements, making it a versatile tool in the IAM landscape.

Examples

  • Connector for LDAP directories to integrate user accounts and groups.
  • Connector for SaaS applications to synchronize user identities and roles.

Additional Information

  • OpenICF is part of the broader identity management community and is often used in conjunction with tools like Apache Syncope and ForgeRock.
  • It is maintained by the OpenICF community, which encourages contributions and collaboration to enhance the framework.
