Atomic swaps

Atomic swaps are a technology that allows the exchange of one cryptocurrency for another without the need for a trusted third party.

Description

Atomic swaps are a method of exchanging cryptocurrencies directly between users without the need for an intermediary or centralized exchange. This process utilizes smart contracts to ensure that the exchange occurs only if both parties fulfill their obligations. The term 'atomic' refers to the fact that the transaction is indivisible; either both parties receive their respective coins or neither does. This is achieved through a mechanism known as Hash Time-Locked Contracts (HTLCs), which enforce the swap conditions and time constraints. If one party fails to fulfill their part of the agreement within the specified timeframe, the funds are returned to the original owners. Atomic swaps promote decentralization and can reduce the need for exchanges, potentially lowering fees and increasing security by minimizing risks associated with custodial services. They can occur on-chain or across different blockchains, enhancing the interoperability of cryptocurrencies and facilitating more efficient trading in the crypto space.

Examples

  • A user wants to trade Bitcoin for Litecoin directly with another user without using an exchange.
  • Two parties agree to swap Ethereum for Ripple using an atomic swap mechanism to ensure a secure transaction.

Additional Information

  • Atomic swaps can occur on different blockchains, enabling cross-chain trading.
  • They help reduce counterparty risk since neither party needs to trust the other to execute the trade.
